Problem: A sports analytics brief asked: beyond population size, which country-level factors best explain Olympic success, and which sports show the most performance inequality across nations?

Key finding: GDP per capita outperforms raw population as a predictor. Countries with GDP per capita above $30k win 3× more medals per capita. Athletics & swimming account for 38% of all medals — high-volume sports where resource investment has outsized returns.

COVID-19 Global Mortality — Separating Signal from Noise

Problem: Raw COVID case counts are misleading without adjusting for population, testing rates, and reporting lag. The goal was to produce a clean SQL-based analytical layer that policy teams could query reliably.

Key finding: Infection fatality rates (IFR) vary 12× across high-income nations — primarily driven by age distribution and vaccination rollout timing, not healthcare capacity rank. Rolling 7-day averages smoothed reporting artefacts that caused 3× spikes in raw data.
